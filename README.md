### Sociological Contents Linked to the Uzbek Telecom Scandal (2010)

1. **Social Structure & Institutions**  
   - Informal power hierarchies replacing formal institutions.  
   - State institutions as façades for elite networks (e.g., telecom bribes for licenses).  

2. **Deviance & Social Control**  
   - **Labeling Theory**: Karimova labeled "deviant" to mask systemic corruption.  
   - **Differential Association**: Officials learned bribery through networks (e.g., customs pyramids).  
   - **Social Control**: Repression (torture, imprisonment) as formal sanction; public acquiescence as informal acceptance.  

3. **Social Inequality & Stratification**  
   - **Global Stratification**: Uzbekistan’s low CPI rank (153rd) reflecting marginalization.  
   - **Class/Power**: Elite wealth (e.g., off-budget funds) vs. forced labor in cotton farms.  
   - **Social Mobility**: Extortion stifling small businesses; foreign investor expropriation.  

4. **Theoretical Perspectives**  
   - **Conflict Theory**: Elite power struggles (Karimova’s downfall as intra-clan rivalry).  
   - **Structural Functionalism**: Corruption’s latent function—stabilizing regime via loyalty bribes.  
   - **Symbolic Interactionism**:  
     - *Impression Management*: Staged trials to project legitimacy.  
     - *Social Construction*: Bribes normalized as "necessary" for bureaucratic navigation.  

5. **Real-Life Incident Analysis**  
   - **Key Actors**: Gulnara Karimova, VimpelCom, TeliaSonera, MTS.  
   - **Legal Fallout**: FCPA fines ($795M–$965M); Swiss asset freezes.  
   - **Global Implications**: Western corporations exploiting weak governance.
